Triton Bikes

Challenge

UC San Diego is exploring ways to promote alternative transportation on campus. Our client, UC San Diego's Transportation Services, requested a bike rental system on campus to promote alternative transportation.

Solution

The proposed solution is a simple 4-step process to check out and return a Triton Bike. The physical system is further complemented with a mobile app that provides additional features to receive discounts and to identify other bike stations.

Understanding the Stakeholder: UCSD Bike Shop

The Bike shop thrives from bike rentals and purchases. Additionally, it maintains Transportation Services' bikes at discounted prices. In light of this, the client does not want the Triton Bike Rental System to ruin their current relationship.

In response to the client'a concerns, I interviewed the Bike Shop's manager.

Surprising Insights:

The Bike Shop is willing to provide bikes to the Triton Rental Bike System--free of charge
After 2 years, the Bike Shop will reclaim the bikes and sell them as used bikes
In exchange, the Bike Shop will be in charge of the bikes' maintenance at set prices
Then it will provide new bikes to the Triton Rental Bike System again and the cycle continues

What Does that Mean?

The Bike Shop = Unlimited Resource Provider
The Relationship between the Bike Shop and Transportation Services will crumble strengthen

Now Let's Understand the Users

4 contextual interviews

with students to learn about their travelling and biking experiences on campus

"I hate getting across campus!"

There is usually a ten-minute timeframe for students to travel to their next class. For classes that are located on opposite ends of the campus, students have to rush there before their class begins. There are times when they arrive to these classes late. For the times when they are not late, they become fatigued from rushing to class.

"Renting a bike is not worth it."

Although some students know of bike rental options, they would rather walk across campus or take the bus shuttle instead of renting a bike. After diving into their reasons, I found out that the minimum rental period is one full day, when students often only need it to go between classes (10 minutes). This lack of flexibility renders bike rental options unappealing.

As Well as Their Environment

Based on the findings, I went with a partner to observe the students' environment during class transitions. We pinpointed potential bike stations by exploring open areas near highly concentrated classroom locations and determining whether constructing bike stations in those spaces would disrupt student traffic flow.
