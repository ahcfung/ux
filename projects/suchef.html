SuChef

Challenge

Currently, cooking apps are one-dimensional and inconsiderate of users' context. They satisfy the minimum requirements of displaying a list of ingredients, directions and some images of the dish. However, the cooking environment is much more complex than simply interacting with a static recipe. With this in mind, I led an independent project with the goal of developing an application to better support home cooks.

Solution

This project is currently in the design phrase, and the solution will be coming in a few weeks. As of now, we are exploring a potential design that leverages Google Glass, a hands-free wearable interface. Stay tuned for the prototype!

Interaction

Current technologies do not satisfy home cooks' basic needs. These users often need help in changing proportions of the ingredients based on their inventory, converting measurement units, and keeping track of the cooking time.
Some home cooks also tend to customize their recipes. These users like to incorporate other people's recommendations into their cooking, adjust the ingredients based on their taste, and substitute ingredients that they don't have.
With their messy hands, home cooks prefer to rely on their memory to retain the long list of recipe directions--which frequently turns disastrous. There is also a lack of visual aids to provide feedback to confirm that they are doing it right.
By looking at the directions as a whole before starting to cook, they become familiar with the overall recipe and can better prepare for the cooking process. Nonetheless, it is often difficult for them to find where they are in the cooking process based on a static recipe list.
