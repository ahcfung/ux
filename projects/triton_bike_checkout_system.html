Triton Bike Checkout System

Portfolio > Triton Bike Checkout System

Overview

Global TIES is a program that puts multi-disciplinary teams of undergraduates to innovate engineering solutions for nonprofit organizations. UC San Diego's Transportation Services approached the program director, requesting for a bike rental system on campus to promote alternative transportation. As a team leader, I led 5 other students from various academic disciplines in conceptualizing a practical solution while integrating some core user-centered techniques.

Methods

We began by interviewing the client and the Bike Shop to understand their needs, capacities and constraints. Next, we derived some of the current breakdowns in travelling around campus using user interviews and field observations, which enabled us to determine strategic bike stations and system requirements. Moreover, I utilized Pencil to prototype mobile wireframes and storyboarded a typical use-case scenario in using the check out station.

Solution

My team envisioned an automatic checkout system that involves 4 simple steps: slide the UCSD ID to check out a bike, ride the bike around campus, slide the ID to unlock a bike rack, and park the bike to end the rental period. To achieve such simplicity while fulfilling secondary features such as identifying a bike station's fullness and the user's rental history, we augmented the system with a mobile application.
