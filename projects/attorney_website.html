Rorem Law

Challenge

Many attorneys and firms have websites to advertise their presence to the public. To keep up with his competitors, a sole practitioner approached my team. He requested a website that will attract more clients while promoting his presence online.

Solution

The end product is a website prototype that fulfills both the client's and his target users' needs. Some notable features include an omnipresent contact form that encourages potential users to reach out to our client and a work process page to establish trust with users who are unfamiliar with law

Roles

  • Project Lead
  • User Experience Researcher
  • User Experience Designer

Understand the Client

We began by gathering the initial requirements using a client survey. The survey helped us to understand the client's work domain as well as his goals for the website.
In a nutshell, the client:
  • is involved with 4 types of legal cases: people with loans, wrongful termination, breach of contract, construction defects. This information defines our target audience.
  • currently markets himself through word of mouth and referrals. We see this as an opportunity to leverage upon a referral section to further build on his status and credibility.
  • had clients who are solely Spanish speakers, so there is a need to have a Spanish translation for all of the content to attract more Spanish-speaking clients.

Understand the Competitors

After scanning through more than ten websites, we analyzed 3 good attorney websites as a means to understand our client's competitors' strengths and weaknesses. The analysis is based on 5 categories: navigation, design, brand, site architecture, and content.

Understand the Target Audience

Although we were only required to conduct a regular survey interview, I introduced to the team the idea and strengths of contextual inquiry. This method helped us understand users' perceptions of the client's competitors as well as enabling the team to dive into our users' implicit needs.

Conceptualizing the Designs

Based on all of the data that we've gathered, we generated the site architecture and brainstormed design ideas. I then converted the team's ideas into wireframes.
