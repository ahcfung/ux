Notifye

Challenge

Hospitals embody a complex environment where nurses labor through extensive shifts to provide patient care. With the advent of Google Glass, a wearable computer interface, my team sought to prototype an application to support this medical community.

Solution

The resulting prototype is a notification and communication system that eliminates some of the interruptive calls from the nurses' station. Moreover, the system takes into account of medication error prevention by delivering notifications only if nurses are not engaged in safety-critical tasks.

Roles

  • User Experience Researcher
  • User Experience Designer
    (for the 2nd iteration)

Understand Nurses' Activities

At the start of the project, I conducted contextual inquiries with two nurses. Due to the privacy limitation of a hospital setting, I set up a location elsewhere and gathered data by having them reflect on their experiences. This data yielded sequence models (a step-by-step process of their activities) as well as some important issues that they encounter during a work shift.

Findings and Progress

Inefficient Communication
Endless Unfiltered Calls
Nurses have to manually check the computer for new doctor's orders. These manual actions are not only time-consuming, but it also takes the nurses' attention away from patients. It further poses a safety risk when a doctor issues a life-critical order and the nurse is too busy to check and execute the order in time.
Nurses receive calls from different people and various departments--from the blood bank that informs them of abnormal lab values to worrying family members who want updates on a patient. These calls are despised interruptions and reach nurses as though they are all equally urgent.
After informing the team of the findings, I contributed to the ideation of the design concept. The Main User Experience Designer of the team then converted these ideas into wireframes.
