We Have, We Need

Portfolio > We Have, We Need

Overview

After Haiti experienced a major earthquake that victimized many of its citizens, nonprofit organizations had no efficient means to communicate with each other to acquire the supplies necessary to aid with disaster relief. Instead, they ordered supplies from their headquarters in their home countries, which often took months to fulfill. In light of this issue, I joined an ongoing team in developing a software that enables nonprofit organizations to request supplies from each other.

Methods

I became accustomed to the developing software by conducting a heuristic evaluation. There, I noticed a flaw: users are motivated to request for supplies, but are not motivated to post their own supplies for others. After conducting subsequent usability testing, the team finally decided to shift direction towards an inventory management system. I helped to design page wireframes using Axure and worked with the UI team to implement the new design.

Solution

My area of focus is the inventory management section. The user's inventory is separated from their requests. Additionally, context-sensitive options to add, remove and transfer items appear based on the user's interaction with the data. Lastly, I integrated graphics to convey feedback on any data changes.
