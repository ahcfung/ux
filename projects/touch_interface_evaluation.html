Touch Interface Evaluation

Portfolio > Touch Interface Evaluation

Overview

The San Diego Red Cross Headquarters had contracted a company to design a new touch technology to improve its disaster support system within the San Diego County. I started out as a user acceptance tester, but moved into the role of a user experience researcher after noticing that my peers were experiencing recurring usability problems.

Methods

I conducted an informal think-aloud protocol on the other user acceptance testers by having them speak aloud their thoughts as they attempted to complete the required tasks. After the session, I spent time alone with the interface to critique it for further usability errors.

Solution

I compiled a report combining my findings and suggestions, then sent it to the head of the disaster support department, who then passed it to the software team. Many minor usability errors were corrected in the final version of the touch interface. However, due to the limitation of funding and time, some of the major usability errors were unable to be resolved as they required an entire interface redesign. Nonetheless, the software team expressed gratitude as they have learned a great deal about usability from my report.
